This morning I was thinking about how there are at least three possibilities for my future concerning the possibility of me eventually dying.

The first of those possibilities is that I don’t actually end up ever dying (whether or not people other than myself perceive me eventually dying).

The second of those possibilities is that I do eventually die but end up getting reincarnated in a new body and possibly in a new universe (whether or not memories, personality traits, and physiological characteristics from one incarnation are carried over into the next incarnation).

The third of those possibilities is that I do eventually die but end up never getting reincarnated.

My hope is that the first of those possibilities happens because that would seemingly entail that I undergo the least amount of identity loss (including the loss of progress I made shaping my encompassing universe, bodily vessel, and mind via my own voluntary efforts and my own involuntary experiences). Such a possibility would entail that I do not lose continuity of consciousness and the sense of being localized to exactly one information processing agent at all times (following the emergence of my selfhood as that conscious information processing agent).

What seems most realistic to me is that the third of those possibilities happens. Assuming that is the most likely outcome, it seems logical for me to prioritize enjoying the finite amount of time I have in which to exist rather than prioritize preparing for future incarnations or for a life which never ends. That would entail that what I consciously invest resources in building during my lifetime is primarily (if not exclusively) for my own amusement and comfort rather than for benefitting people other than myself or for benefitting incarnations of myself other than my present incarnation. That is because it seems impossible for me to verify whether or not loci of conscious experience other than myself (as my present incarnation) ever exist throughout nature. Hence, I might as well prioritize the one and only locus of conscious experience which I know to exist: my own frame of reference.

I feel compelled to treat the second possibility functionally the same as I would treat the third possibility. That is because the second possibility entails that there may be some loss of structural integrity of my ego (and what that ego constructs externally to itself in its encompassing universe as some kind of art form or tangible legacy) between successive incarnations. Hence, each time I die would more or less seem to me like an erasure of my identity and the ledger of accomplishments, transgressions, and ethically neutral actions my identity was responsible for enacting.
